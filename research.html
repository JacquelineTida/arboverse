Diagnostic, genomics and pathogenesis of arboviruses

Quote about the subject

Arboviral diseases are infections transmitted by the bite of infected hematophagous arthropods. Arboviruses such as dengue, yellow fever, West Nile, Chikungunya and Zika disproportionately affect most vulnerable populations, causing hundreds of millions of cases annually and overwhelming health systems in tropical countries. Our work has been developing diagnosis assays (serological and molecular) and characterized genomic diversity of ~50 arbovirus species from tropical regions of the Americas. In addition, we have to combine epidemiological, clinical and genomic data to investigate new characteristics of endemic arboviruses, such as fatal outcomes caused by the Chikungunya virus.

Virus Discovery and Evolution

Quote about the subject

Combining metagenomics and bioinformatics approaches, we have been discovered and characterized more than 100 novel viruses in samples from rodents, bats, opossums, avian and ticks from South America, Africa and Antarctica. Also, we have been conducting studies of evolutionary history of emerging viruses, including element viruses endogenous originated and integrated animal genomes in hundreds to millions of years ago.

SARS-CoV-2 and COVID-19

Quote about the subject

During the ongoing COVID-19 epidemic, we conducted several studies about SARS-CoV-2 and COVID-19 in Brazil. Based on large joint analyses of genomic and epidemiological data, we reported >100 international virus introductions in Brazil and uncovered temporal changes in the patterns of virus spread and transmission across the country. Also, we identified that socioeconomic disparities dictate access to COVID-19 diagnosis and that heterogeneously implemented non-pharmaceutical interventions impede epidemic control. At the same time, we reported that low-income and Black and Pardo communities are more likely to die with COVID-19. We contributed with the identification of P.1 lineage that emerged in Manaus city. Subsequently, we showed that P.1 lineage might escape from neutralizing antibodies elicited by previous SARS-CoV-2 infection and chemically-inactivated vaccine.
